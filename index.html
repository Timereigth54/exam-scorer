How scoring works

Every response here is read and scored by Claude (Anthropic's AI) — but it isn't grading freely. For each exam, it's instructed to follow specific scoring criteria sourced from real exam materials, not its own general sense of "good writing."

Where each rubric comes from

IELTS — IELTS's own band descriptor framework: Task Achievement / Task Response, Coherence and Cohesion, Lexical Resource, and Grammatical Range and Accuracy.
TOEFL iBT — ETS's official scoring criteria for the current TOEFL format (the Write an Email / Academic Discussion and Listen and Repeat / Take an Interview tasks introduced in January 2026).
Duolingo English Test — Duolingo's own published scoring dimensions: Content, Discourse Coherence, Grammar, and Lexis. DET's full scoring algorithm is proprietary and undisclosed, so practice scores here are our best grounded approximation, not an official DET prediction.
Cambridge FCE, CAE, CPE, PET, KET — Cambridge Assessment English's official Handbook for Teachers for each individual level, including examiner guidance on common mistakes.
ОГЭ and ЕГЭ — FIPI's official current exam specification and demo version (демоверсия) for the Russian state exams.

Every rubric above has been reviewed and approved by Kay, an experienced English teacher across these exams — not generated by AI guesswork. This explains our methodology; it isn't a guarantee of your real exam score, but it's grounded in the same standards real examiners use.
